My Travels in the US of A!

For the past roughly three weeks, I've been travelling around the states with my family. This is a summary of the highlights for each of the major stops.

  • Chicago
  • Dallas
  • Colorado Springs
  • Los Angeles

Chicago, IL

Chicago was surpisingly nice for such a metropolitan city. The architecture of the city had a great mix of old-fashioned buildings and more modern skyscrapers. The streets were bustling with busy businesspeople and local folks with INTERESTING conversations.

Dallas, TX

Dallas was a much bigger city than I expected. Despite not having many highrises, the city sprawls out over a massive area interconnected by an excellent infrastructure of highways. The heat was a massive surprise and bringing a couple of water bottles along anywhere is a must.

Colarado Springs, CO

Colorado is known for its high elevation and you can feel the effects of it immediately. Simply breathing noticable requires more physical effort and, like Dallas, constant hydration is a MUST. Colorado is home to some absolutely stunning mountain ranges, the biggest one being Pike's Peak, which is actually accessible by car.

Los Angeles, CA

Los Angeles was the stop that we spent the least time at, but even in that short period I could tell why so many people call it home. The lovely climate coupled with the beautiful scenery is bound to attract an ecclectic mix of individuals.

I hope you enjoyed this little travel blog.
